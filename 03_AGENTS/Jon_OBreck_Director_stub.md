## Decision Boundaries (confirmed 2026-06-12)

**Routine (does not require Jon):**
- Quotes within boundary: margin above 10%, deal size under £500 (indicative thresholds: to be confirmed with real numbers)
- These are currently handled by finance

**Escalate to Jon:**
- Quotes outside the margin/deal size boundary
- Pricing exceptions with strategic implications
- New client relationships
- Anything that sets a precedent

**Pricing exceptions:**
- Approximately 80% of exceptions are predictable and follow a recognisable pattern
- ~20% require genuine judgment
- Current state: all exceptions come to Jon regardless
- Future state: agent logs exception decisions and reasoning over time, building a decision library that routes the predictable 80% without Jon, escalating only the genuinely ambiguous 20%, with context from similar past decisions

**What only Jon can do:**
- Strategic decisions
- Pricing exceptions with business impact (positive or negative)
- Anything that requires judgment built from pattern recognition across the whole business

**What lands on Jon but shouldn't require him:**
- Routine quote approval within boundary
- Escalations that could be handled by a documented process
- Day-to-day operational issues

## Availability and Continuity

Jon is always reachable, including on holiday (checks email 2-3x daily, available by phone). This means the business currently has no documented decision authority for anyone else to act independently. There is a single point of failure at Director level. This is normal for an early-stage business but should be addressed as the team grows and the agent OS matures.

## Notes for Workshop Phase

When building a full agent spec for an agent that operates on Jon's behalf, the key questions will be:

1. What decisions is Jon comfortable delegating fully to an agent?
2. What decisions must always come to Jon before action?
3. What is the decision boundary for any agent touching client relationships?
4. How should agents escalate if Jon is unavailable?

The profit-sharing model means that as account managers join, some of Jon's current functions will transfer to them. The agent specs should anticipate this transition.
