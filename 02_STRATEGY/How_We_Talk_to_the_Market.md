## Summary

GOURD Freight's voice is direct, confident, and quietly irreverent: the voice of people who've spent years watching the logistics industry waste money and carbon, and decided to do something about it. This document governs how that voice is expressed across all customer-facing and market-facing communication.

## Context

GOURD Freight speaks with unusual clarity for a freight forwarder. "Relax. We're tracking it." is a tagline that does a lot of work: it signals confidence, reliability, and a technology backbone without needing to explain any of it. The about page calls the team "Freight Heroes" in the same breath as "old-fashioned service-oriented folk": that combination of self-deprecation and genuine pride is distinctive.

The target audience includes businesses that care about their carbon footprint (B Corp folk are explicitly mentioned as likely to care most), supply chain managers frustrated by opacity and delays, and account managers from other forwarders who are unhappy with the support their employer provides.

## Brand Pillars

1. **Reliability with visibility.** "Relax. We're tracking it." The promise is not just that freight moves, but that the client always knows where it is and why.
2. **Sustainability without moralising.** GOURD Freight talks about carbon reduction in practical terms: kilograms saved, percentage reductions, cost of compute honestly acknowledged. Not hectoring, just information.
3. **Technology that works like a person.** The AI does the admin; the humans do the thinking. The technology is never the hero of the story.
4. **Service as a standard.** "We bend over backwards to make it happen. Always." This is not a differentiator: it's a minimum standard.

## Tone and Voice

- **Direct.** Short sentences. Concrete numbers where possible. "1 Gallon of fuel will move your goods 246 miles by rail compared with only 88 miles by road."
- **Warm but not fluffy.** The company clearly has personality, but it's the personality of people who get things done, not a lifestyle brand.
- **Honest about trade-offs.** When something has a cost, including AI compute, say so. The market respects this.
- **Practical sustainability.** Never preach. Give numbers. Let the client decide.

The company uses "clients" not "customers" in several places. That language should be consistent.

## Audience Segmentation

| Audience | Primary Need | Messaging Approach |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Supply chain / logistics managers at SMEs and mid-market businesses | Reliability, cost efficiency, carbon reporting for internal ESG targets | Lead with tracking, reliability, and carbon reporting as a bundled proposition |
| B Corp companies or businesses with active sustainability goals | Reducing Scope 3 emissions from logistics, provable carbon savings | Lead with the Carbon Healthcheck, modal shift data, and measurable outcomes |
| Senior account managers at other freight forwarders | Better support, better technology, better income model | Lead with the profit-sharing model and the "admin assistant that never sleeps" framing |
| Importers and exporters needing multimodal freight | Route and rate comparison across air, sea, road, rail | Lead with the breadth of modes and the route optimisation capability |

## Key Claims and Evidence Requirements

- "We use AI to reduce planning wastage and inaccuracy" → Evidence: route optimisation case studies (OnBoard Courier, Engine Delivery examples on site)
- "Rail provides up to 76% carbon reduction on an equivalent road journey" → Evidence: cited DfT source; should always be cited
- "99% reliability since 2006" (re: leading rail freight operator) → Evidence: RSSB source cited; use carefully: this is a third-party stat, not GOURD Freight's own
- "We track everything: rates, shipments, speed, emissions" → Evidence: platform capability; should be demonstrable via client-facing tracking

- UK advertising standards (ASA/CAP): environmental claims must be substantiated and not misleading
