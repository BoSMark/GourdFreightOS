## Summary

GOURD Freight competes in the UK freight forwarding market as a technology-enabled, sustainability-focused forwarder targeting clients who want more than the cheapest rate. This document captures the competitive landscape and where GOURD Freight is best positioned to win.

## Context

UK freight forwarding is a large, fragmented market dominated by global players (DB Schenker, Kuehne+Nagel, DHL Global Forwarding, Panalpina/DSV) and populated by hundreds of smaller independent forwarders. GOURD Freight is a small, recently founded forwarder differentiating on technology, carbon focus, and service quality.

The company is a BIFA member, which provides credibility, access to standard trading conditions, and industry network. It operates from Ipswich, Suffolk: a useful location given proximity to Felixstowe, the UK's largest container port.

## Market Category

Technology-enabled sustainable freight forwarding. GOURD Freight positions at the intersection of three things most incumbents don't combine well: digital booking and tracking, genuine carbon expertise, and personal service.

## Positioning Statement

For UK businesses, especially those with sustainability goals, who are frustrated by impersonal service from large forwarders and lack of genuine carbon data from smaller ones, GOURD Freight is the freight forwarder that combines real-time tracking, measurable emissions reduction, and the personal service of people who "bend over backwards." Unlike global players who use sustainability as a marketing layer, GOURD Freight's carbon reporting is built into every quote and every route plan.

## Competitive Strengths

- **Specificity of sustainability claims.** Citing DfT data, acknowledging AI carbon cost, targeting B Corp companies: this is credible in a way that most forwarders' sustainability pages are not.
- **Profit-sharing model.** Creates a talent pipeline that is structurally different from the market. An experienced account manager with a £1m book who joins GOURD Freight is immediately accretive.
- **Multimodal plus carbon.** Air, sea, road, rail, and carbon reporting on all of them. Most smaller forwarders don't have this breadth combined with genuine sustainability capability.
- **Honest language.** "Relax. We're tracking it." and "old-fashioned service-oriented folk" are more honest and more memorable than most freight marketing.

## Competitive Vulnerabilities

- **Small and young.** Incorporated November 2023. Some clients will prefer established names, especially for high-value or time-critical shipments.
- **Technology claims need substantiation.** The website describes AI, route optimisation, and ERP integration at a level that larger forwarders also claim. The differentiation needs to be demonstrable, not just stated.
- **Dependence on founder.** At this stage, the business likely relies heavily on the founder's relationships and judgment. The five-year exit goal requires reducing this dependence systematically.
- **B Corp not yet certified.** On the journey, but not there yet. Competitors could claim this first.
