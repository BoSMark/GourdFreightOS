## Summary

GOURD Freight grows by attracting clients who care about both cost and carbon, and account managers who want to do better work with better tools. This document captures the commercial model and the logic behind how the business scales.

## Context

GOURD Freight is an early-stage freight forwarder incorporated in November 2023. Based on public information, the company is small (5 employees per some sources), with a Ipswich HQ and a full multimodal capability: air, sea, road, rail, storage, and customs. The business has a distinctive dual growth model: winning clients directly, and attracting experienced account managers from other forwarders under a profit-sharing arrangement.

The five-year horizon goal is to build the business to a point where the founder has informed options, which means the OS needs to be built for transferable clarity, documented decisions, and a business that can operate without being dependent on any single person.

## Go-To-Market Model

GOURD Freight's model is hybrid:
- **Service-led for clients:** Clients book freight via a digital platform (ERP integration, EDI, or online booking). The account relationship is human-led with technology in the back office.
- **Account manager recruitment as a growth channel:** The careers page explicitly targets experienced account managers who own existing client books. The profit-sharing model (50% of operating profit from their book) is designed to attract people who know they generate value but aren't being rewarded for it.

**Confirmed (2026-06-12):** No account managers are currently on the profit-sharing model. This remains a planned channel, not yet active.

This is an unusual model in UK freight forwarding. It means growth can come from hiring the right people, not just from marketing spend.

## North Star Metric

**Confirmed (2026-06-12):** Monthly active clients: clients who placed at least one booking in the last 30 days.

**Current baseline:** 500 monthly active clients.

This metric is directionally right but has a known limitation: it treats a client who books once a year the same as one who books every week. The business is moving toward a two-signal model when data infrastructure supports it:

- **Depth:** average shipments per active client per month (captures growing clients)
- **Retention:** percentage of last month's active clients who booked again this month (captures exclusive clients)

Until then, monthly active clients remains the primary reported number.

## Primary Revenue Stream

Freight forwarding margin: the difference between carrier rates and the rates charged to clients. Additionally, value-added services (route optimisation, carbon reporting, sustainability consultancy via Carbon Healthcheck). The free Carbon Healthcheck is likely a loss leader that generates client relationships.

## Growth Thesis

1. **Win clients who care about both cost and carbon.** The addressable market narrows but deepens here: these clients tend to be stickier, more sophisticated, and more likely to refer. B Corp businesses, businesses with Scope 3 reporting obligations, and ESG-conscious supply chain teams are the priority segment.
2. **Attract account managers with existing client relationships.** One experienced account manager with a £500k-£2m book is worth more than a large marketing budget. The profit-sharing model is designed to make this compelling.
3. **Grow recurring, contract logistics relationships.** Spot bookings are fine but the contract logistics offering (for ongoing relationships) is likely higher-margin and more predictable.
4. **Build the platform as a competitive moat.** ERP integration, EDI, real-time tracking, emissions reporting: these integrations create switching costs. Once a client's systems talk to GOURD Freight's, the relationship deepens.

## Pricing Philosophy

GOURD Freight's pricing is not publicly detailed, but the model signals:
- Rates competitive enough to win on price for clients who care
- The profit-sharing model for account managers means pricing must sustain a 50% operating margin split, which disciplines the commercial model
- The Carbon Healthcheck is free, signalling a consultative rather than transactional relationship with the sustainability offer

**Confirmed (2026-06-12):** Margins are better than industry standard. Specific figures not recorded here.

## Personal Goal Context

The founder's goal is to be in a position to have informed options and exit the business within five years. This shapes growth priorities:
- The business needs to be able to operate without the founder's personal client relationships
- Processes and systems need to be documented and transferable
- Growth metrics need to be trackable by any future owner or buyer
- The agent OS itself is a key part of making the business legible and transferable
