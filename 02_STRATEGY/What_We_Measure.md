## Summary

This document defines what GOURD Freight tracks, how often, and why. Given the founder's goal of building towards an informed exit in five years, the measurement framework must produce numbers a future owner or buyer can trust.

## North Star Metric

**Working candidate:** Monthly Active Clients, the number of clients who placed at least one booking in the past 30 days.

This was identified by the founder as the most meaningful signal of healthy business direction: growth in user activity month on month. Monthly Active Clients is a concrete proxy for this.

[INTERNAL: The Director should confirm whether Monthly Active Clients is the right North Star or whether Shipment Volume or Monthly Booking Value is more meaningful. The key test: if this number is growing, is the business unambiguously heading in the right direction?]

## Five-Year Exit Context

For the founder's goal of building towards an informed exit, the measurement framework should also track:

- **Business independence from founder**: what % of client relationships are managed by people other than the founder?
- **Documented revenue predictability**: recurring/contract logistics as % of total revenue
- **Operating margin**: what does the business earn after costs, before founder remuneration?
- **B Corp status**: certification is a signal of quality and values to potential buyers

[INTERNAL: These "exit readiness" metrics should be reviewed annually with the explicit question: "Is this business now legible and transferable to a buyer who doesn't know the founder personally?"]
