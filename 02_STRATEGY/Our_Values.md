## Summary

GOURD Freight was founded on a conviction that the logistics industry wastes too much, in carbon, in human effort, in poor planning, and that technology now exists to fix it. This document captures the values that flow from that founding belief and how they govern the business.

## Context

GOURD Freight Ltd was incorporated in November 2023 in Ipswich, Suffolk. The company describes itself as "old-fashioned service-oriented folk, using lots of modern technology." That tension (the warmth of a reliable human service provider combined with the rigour of AI-driven planning) is the core of the brand identity and the values framework.

The founding belief, stated plainly on the website: "We believe that delays in every aspect of a booking cause waste. We've witnessed too much waste in logistics. Human errors, poor planning, repeated movements, high-carbon carriers chosen when better planning could have meant lowest-carbon transport."

The company is on a journey towards B Corp certification, which signals that environmental and social impact are not marketing claims but structural commitments.

## Core Values

**1. Waste is wrong.** This runs through everything: wasted carbon, wasted time, wasted movement, wasted money. It's not just an efficiency argument; it's an ethical one. When a shipment takes a high-carbon route because someone didn't have the data to choose better, that's a failure worth fixing.

**2. Service before self.** The careers page says it directly: "We bend over backwards to make it happen. Always." The proposition to account managers is profit-sharing rather than a fixed salary, which puts the company's interests and the customer's interests in alignment.

**3. Technology in service of people.** GOURD Freight uses AI to plan faster than a human can, but the company is explicit that "Freight Heroes" are humans backed by technology, not the other way around. The customer calls a knowledgeable person; the AI does the admin.

**4. Transparency about trade-offs.** The emissions reduction page is honest about the carbon cost of AI compute, then explains why it's still net positive. This kind of trade-off transparency is unusual in the industry and should be a defining characteristic of how GOURD Freight communicates.

**5. The right time, not just in time.** The website makes a pointed argument against "Just in Time" as a dogma: "'at the right time' will suffice and prevent unnecessary movements." This is a values position, not just an operational one: it pushes back against a model that has caused supply chain fragility and environmental damage.

## Governing Framework

- **Ethical Principles:** Waste reduction as a moral obligation; transparency about environmental impact; service as a professional standard, not a differentiator
- **Stakeholder Model:** Customers first (their cargo, their carbon, their reliability); team members second (profit-sharing model, autonomy, "you're a grown-up"); then the environment as a genuine third stakeholder, not an afterthought
- **Decision Framework:** When speed conflicts with sustainability, GOURD Freight should present the trade-off honestly and let the client decide with full information. When service quality conflicts with margin, service wins.
- **B Corp Journey:** The company has stated it is working towards B Corp certification. This constrains and guides decisions across hiring, vendor selection, environmental reporting, and governance.

## Scope and Boundaries

This document governs:
- How values translate into service standards and what we will and won't do for a client
- How environmental commitments are expressed in what routes and carriers we recommend
- How the profit-sharing model is explained and maintained
- How AI use is described to clients and what data we do and don't process

Out of scope:
- Day-to-day operational decisions (those reference this document, not override it)
